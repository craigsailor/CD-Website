What is Cryptographic Democracy?

Cryptographic Democracy is about creating social-structures and distributed entities secured by cryptography.

Why do you need this?

We wanted to establish a foundation, but the existing system did not fit us. It is cumbersome and slow. It was expensive and outdated. It wanted us to be tied to one jurisdiction; though we are digital nomads, spreaded all over the world.

Cryptographic Democracy fix this problem. It is a method to establish and manage organizations with a press of a button: companies, foundations, even states.

Trust in traditional middlemen is replaced by cryptography; Notaries, lawyers and auditors are replaced by digital signatures, hashes and consensus algorithms.

How does it work?

The project is to create a tool, with whom people can register an organization, create and enforce the bylaws in a distributed decentralized manner. It is done in two parts. One is a format to describe organizations. The other is a set of tools to create and manage organizations.
The format describes an organization is a combination of script and cryptographic tools. It looks similar to a blockchain data structure (also known as, hashed linked lists). This unlike legal contracts which are commonly used today.
The organization is managed as a private blockchain (a method to make decisions in closed groups). This gives the organization a distributed identity (equivalent to legal identity of traditional organizations), simplifying participation for all members in the organization and making it very difficult for the organization to be corrupted by a small nefarious group.

Organizations

Whether it's a company a foundation or an insttitute; Cryptographic Democracy allows to manage an organization with an easy way to keep its integrity and transparencncy. Cryptographic Democracy enables easy auditing and avoid situation such as the red-cross Haiti affair.

International efforts

The Paris agreement calls for a “Technology framework” for communication of parties, which shall be “maintained by the secretariat” as a “public registry”. Using Cryptographic Democracy is a base for this framework ensures fair treatment, completeness of data and ease-international interaction between states.
